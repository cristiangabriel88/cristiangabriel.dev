How it works

Three steps. That's the whole product.

QuickPaste was designed to disappear. The first time you use it should feel like nothing happened — which is the point.

01

Select on any page

Highlight a sentence, a paragraph, a code block, or right-click an image. QuickPaste doesn't read your pages — only what you tell it to save.

02

Right-click → Save to QuickPaste

One menu item, two words. Multi-paragraph selections keep their structure. A subtle notification confirms the save (you can turn it off).

Or turn on auto-capture and Ctrl+C saves the clip for you — no menu needed. More →

03

Open the popup whenever

Click the toolbar icon or press AltShiftQ. Your latest clips are there, copy-ready, in chronological order.

New in 2.0

Or skip the right-click entirely. Just press CtrlC.

Flip the Auto-capture toggle in Settings and QuickPaste starts saving every copy you make, automatically — across every tab, on every site, with the source URL attached. It's the same clipboard you already use; QuickPaste just remembers what was on it.

  • Off by default. One checkbox in Settings turns it on. One unchecks it.
  • Per-domain blocklist. Add mail.google.com, your bank, your password manager — copies on those sites are ignored.
  • Smart dedupe. Mash Ctrl+C three times on the same selection? Still one clip.
  • Never leaves your device. The capture script writes through the same local storage as everything else — no upload, no telemetry.

Two views, one collection

The popup for now. The Collection for everything.

The toolbar popup is for the clip you saved twenty seconds ago. The Collection page is for the one you saved last March. They share the same storage — so anything you save is in both, instantly.

The popup

Glance. Copy. Carry on.

Open it with AltShiftQ or the toolbar icon. Your most recent clips are at the top, with one-click copy, pin, edit, label, and send-to actions tucked into each card.

  • Filter tabs — All, Pinned, Labeled, or clips with a source URL.
  • Quick save — Tap the + to type or paste a clip directly into the popup.
  • Inline edit — Fix a typo without ever leaving the popup.
The collection

Where the rest of your library lives.

The Collection page opens in its own tab — a calmer, fuller view that adds bulk selection, advanced filters, drag-and-drop reorder, saved searches, settings, and import/export. Same clips, more room.

  • Bulk select & act — Per-clip checkboxes plus Select All. Delete with a 6-second Undo toast.
  • Drag-and-drop reorder in manual sort mode, or sort by newest, oldest, or A–Z.
  • Bulk copy with format — plain, with source URLs, with timestamps, or as Markdown.
  • JSON / Markdown export of the whole collection, and import to bring it back.
Organize

Built for clipboards that actually grow.

Pins for the clips you reuse weekly. Labels for the ones you only need during one project. Drag-to-reorder for everything else. Three lightweight ideas that compose into a system you can keep up with.

Pin the essentials

Pinned clips sit at the top with a soft green border. They survive auto-prune and ride along with optional sync.

Label per clip

Add free-form labels via the tag-icon button. A "Pick from existing" row suggests labels you've already used, so you stay consistent.

Sort or arrange

Newest first, oldest first, A–Z, or full manual order — drag any clip to a new position and the order sticks.

Edit in place

Fix a typo, merge two paragraphs, or rewrite the wording. Edits save instantly to local storage — no Save button.

Dedupe smartly

Strict, normalized (case + whitespace insensitive), or off. Catches the "I clipped this twice last week" problem.

Auto-prune (opt-in)

Keep clips for 7, 30, or 90 days. Pinned clips are never pruned. Off by default — you decide your own retention.

Cap the collection

Set a soft ceiling at 100, 500, or 1000 clips. Oldest unpinned drop first; your pins stay safe. Off by default.

Saved searches

Name a query+filter combo and recall it with one click. Stop re-typing label:research after:7d.

Find anything

Search that forgives your typos.

Fuzzy matching means recevied still finds received. Match highlighting shows exactly where your query landed. Filter by label or date range. Save any combination as a one-click saved search.

  • Live, fuzzy — results update as you type, with edit-distance tolerance.
  • Date filters — today, last 7/30 days, or a custom range.
  • Saved searches — name a query+filter combo and recall it with one click.
Send to

Move a clip out of QuickPaste in one click.

Saved clips don't live in a silo. Push them to the tools you already use, in the format that tool expects.

N

Notion

Copies the clip to your clipboard and opens a new Notion page in a new tab. One Ctrl+V and you're done.

G

Google Docs

Same pattern — copies the clip, opens a fresh Doc. No OAuth dance, no API keys, no surface area for the extension to abuse.

Looks and feel

Light, dark, and made to be lived in.

Real dark mode (not just an inverted palette). A customisable accent colour. Compact or comfortable density. Toggle the source-URL line. Adjustable preview length. The UI bends to your reading habits, not the other way around.

  • Fraunces · Inter · Sora — a serif for headings, a sans for body, and a humanist sans for buttons.
  • System-aware theme that respects prefers-color-scheme on first launch.
  • Reduced-motion respectful — the floating logo holds still when you ask it to.
Privacy

Your clips never leave your machine. Period.

QuickPaste has no servers and no analytics. The optional Chrome Sync mirror uses your Google account — the developer has no access to your data. The <all_urls> permission exists so the right-click menu works on every site and so the opt-in Ctrl+C auto-capture can listen for your own copy events when you enable it. The extension does not read page content on its own.

  • No account
  • No telemetry
  • No third-party scripts
  • Open source
  • Local-first by default

The origin

A Harvard CS50 project that just kept growing.

v1.0 started as a CS50x final project — friends had asked for "something fast and easy, to select some text and save it, like taking notes as you go, but no logging in, no account, really simple and fast." That brief is still the north star.

v2.0 (2026) was a near-complete rewrite around the same core idea, with the things four years of using it taught us. The headline addition: opt-in Ctrl+C auto-capture with a per-domain blocklist, so you never have to remember to right-click. Plus image clips, pins, labels, drag-and-drop, fuzzy search, in-place edit, bulk operations with undo, Chrome Sync, send-to integrations, JSON/Markdown import/export, retention rules, and a proper light/dark theme.

Build something of interest to you, that solves an actual problem, that impacts your community. — CS50x final project brief
